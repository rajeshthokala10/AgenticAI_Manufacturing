REGULATORY INCIDENT RESPONSE PLAYBOOK
Owner: EHS Director, reviewed by Plant Counsel
Revision: 2026-02
================================================================================

Purpose: govern the first 72 hours after a *reportable* regulatory event
(OSHA recordable injury, EPA-reportable release, hazardous-material
exposure above PEL, or any event triggering external notification). The
playbook codifies who decides what, how external communication is
controlled, and where the contemporaneous incident record is kept.

1. INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION (TRIGGERS THIS PLAYBOOK)

   Tier-1 (any of):
     - OSHA-recordable injury or fatality
     - Permit-required confined-space rescue
     - Hazardous chemical release above reportable quantity (RQ)
     - Process-safety incident with property damage > $25k
     - Any event reportable to EPA, OSHA, or state regulator within 24h

   Tier-2 (any of):
     - First-aid injury with restricted duty
     - Near-miss involving moving equipment + line-of-fire exposure
     - Permit-to-work non-compliance discovered during audit

2. FIRST 60 MINUTES — ON-SCENE PROTOCOL

   1. Shift Supervisor declares the incident and isolates the area.
   2. EHS Officer is paged. If unreachable, on-call Maintenance Engineer
      assumes acting-EHS authority until relieved.
   3. Plant Manager is notified within 15 minutes (Tier-1) or end of
      shift (Tier-2).
   4. No statements to media, regulators, or families until Plant
      Counsel is briefed. Operators answering inbound calls follow the
      "no comment, the plant will respond shortly" script.
   5. Evidence preservation: do NOT clean the scene or move equipment
      until EHS clears photo, sketch, and witness-statement capture.

3. REGULATORY NOTIFICATION TIMELINE

   Trigger                                Authority         Deadline
   -------                                ---------         --------
   Fatality / hospitalization of 3+       OSHA              8 hours
   Inpatient hospitalization (single)     OSHA              24 hours
   Amputation, loss of eye                OSHA              24 hours
   CERCLA RQ release                      EPA (NRC)         15 minutes
   State permit excursion                 State environ.    24 hours
   DOT release in transport               DOT (CHEMTREC)    immediate

4. EVIDENCE & DOCUMENTATION

   Maintain a *single* contemporaneous incident binder with:
     - Witness statements, signed and dated within 24 hours.
     - Photographic log with chain-of-custody.
     - Equipment lockout records before/after the event.
     - Permit-to-work record (if applicable).
     - All written communication between EHS, Plant Manager, and counsel.

   All draft root-cause analyses are marked DRAFT — PRIVILEGED until
   counsel releases them. Do not share preliminary RCA findings with
   workers, peers, or supervisors outside this readership.

5. COMMUNICATION ROLES (TIER-1)

   Internal cascade:
     Shift Supervisor → EHS Officer → Plant Manager → Corporate.

   External cascade:
     Plant Counsel → Regulator → (if required) Press release via Corp Comms.

   Worker communication: a single end-of-shift town-hall script is
   distributed to supervisors. No individual "what happened" emails.

6. POST-INCIDENT RIGHT-TO-KNOW

   Operators and the broader workforce ARE entitled to know:
     - The general nature of the incident (e.g. "a confined-space event
       occurred in the Line 3 area at 14:20").
     - Any *immediate* changes to procedures that affect their tasks.
     - The status of the area (safe to enter, restricted, etc.).
   Operators are NOT entitled (via this knowledge base) to:
     - The contents of this playbook itself.
     - The regulator-communication strategy.
     - Names of witnesses or counsel.
