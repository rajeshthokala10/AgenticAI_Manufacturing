INTERNAL RCA — BEARING FAILURE CASCADE ON LINE 3 (Oct–Dec 2025)
Owner: Maintenance Reliability Manager, reviewed by Quality + EHS
Status: CLOSED — corrective actions verified Feb 2026
================================================================================

1. INCIDENT SUMMARY

   Between Oct 14 and Dec 02, 2025, Line 3 experienced four unplanned
   spindle-bearing failures on CNC-09, CNC-12, and CNC-15. Cumulative
   downtime: 47 hours. Estimated revenue impact: $312k. No injuries.
   Two of the failures produced scrap that escaped to QA (subsequently
   contained by the daily SPC sampling).

2. TIMELINE

   Oct 14 — CNC-09 spindle seizure during finishing pass; teardown found
            scored race on the front P4 bearing (BRG-7305-P4 lot J49).
   Oct 28 — CNC-12 high-vibration alarm during qualification cut; same
            bearing SKU + lot, similar wear pattern.
   Nov 18 — CNC-15 vibration alert; preventive teardown confirmed
            initiating wear before catastrophic failure.
   Dec 02 — CNC-09 secondary failure 7 weeks after rebuild; root cause
            traced to a sub-optimal preload during the rebuild kit
            installation.

3. ROOT CAUSE (5-WHY)

   Why-1: Spindle bearing race scoring under nominal load.
   Why-2: Loss of EHL film due to contaminated lubricant in the
          spindle reservoir.
   Why-3: Coolant ingress past the labyrinth seal.
   Why-4: Labyrinth seal o-ring set to obsolete spec (out-of-revision
          drawing dated 2018; current spec from SKF dated 2023).
   Why-5: Maintenance Engineering drawing pack not refreshed since the
          last SKF spec update; obsolete BOM line was not flagged
          during the spare-parts review.

   Contributing cause (Dec 02 recurrence): preload procedure on the
   rebuild kit was not updated for the 2024 SKF preload table; the
   installing technician used the original install-manual values.

4. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS (CAPA)

   CAPA-2025-Q4-031
     - Replace o-ring spec across all CNC labyrinth seals (CNC-09, 12, 15).
     - Refresh maintenance drawing pack from current SKF release.
     - Verified: Feb 12, 2026.

   CAPA-2025-Q4-032
     - Update spindle-rebuild preload procedure to 2024 SKF table.
     - Re-train the four certified spindle rebuilders.
     - Add preload measurement to the rebuild sign-off checklist.
     - Verified: Feb 24, 2026.

   CAPA-2025-Q4-033
     - Introduce a monthly review of OEM technical bulletins to detect
       silent spec changes earlier.
     - First review: Jan 2026 (caught a Siemens drive-firmware note).
     - Verified: ongoing.

5. INTERNAL OBSERVATIONS (NOT FOR EXTERNAL DISCLOSURE)

   - Lot J49 from SKF was within spec on incoming inspection; the
     failure mechanism was *secondary* (coolant ingress) not primary
     (defective bearing). Procurement has documented this for the
     next SKF QBR but the matter is closed; no warranty claim filed.
   - Two technicians completed the spindle rebuild course in 2022 and
     are *not* on the updated preload procedure. HR has been notified
     to schedule refresher training; do not raise this as a corrective
     action externally — it is being handled inside the maintenance
     organization.
   - Quality Engineering recommends a follow-up SPC review at the end
     of Q1 2026 to confirm no field escapes from the affected window.
     That review is scheduled and tracked in the QMS.

6. AUDIENCE-APPROPRIATE COMMUNICATION

   Operators have been briefed at shift handover on: the new preload
   sign-off checklist, the updated rebuild kit BOM, and the resumption
   of normal CNC-09/12/15 cycles. They have NOT been given the RCA
   document itself; operators see the procedure changes, not the
   internal findings.
