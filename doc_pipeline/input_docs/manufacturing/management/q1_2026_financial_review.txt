Q1 2026 FINANCIAL REVIEW — Houston Manufacturing Plant
Prepared by: Office of the CFO
Period: Jan 1, 2026 – Mar 31, 2026
================================================================================

1. REVENUE & MARGIN SUMMARY

   Net Plant Revenue ............... $42.7M  (+8.4% YoY, +2.1% vs plan)
   Gross Margin .................... 31.2%   (plan 29.5%, +170 bps)
   EBITDA .......................... $7.9M   (18.5% of revenue)
   Operating Income ................ $5.2M   (12.2% of revenue)
   Free Cash Flow .................. $4.1M

   The margin beat is driven primarily by lower scrap on Line 3 after the
   bearing recall remediation closed in February, and by a deferred
   maintenance benefit on CNC-12 that will reverse in Q2.

2. CAPEX EXECUTION VS APPROVED PLAN

   Approved FY26 capex envelope .... $18.0M
   Committed YTD ................... $4.6M  (26% of plan)
   Cash deployed YTD ............... $2.1M  (12% of plan)
   At-risk capex ................... $1.8M  (CNC spindle replacement
                                              awaiting board sign-off)

   Major capex projects in-flight:
     - Servo drive replacement, Line 2     — $1.4M committed, on schedule
     - Vision system upgrade, QA cell      — $0.9M committed, +6 weeks late
     - Capacity expansion, Line 5          — $2.3M approved, kickoff Q2

3. SUPPLIER COST EXPOSURE (TOP 5)

   #1  SKF North America     Bearings, spindle parts   $3.4M YTD spend
   #2  Siemens Industry      Servo drives, PLCs        $2.1M YTD spend
   #3  Sandvik Coromant      Cutting tools, inserts    $1.8M YTD spend
   #4  Parker Hannifin       Hydraulics, pneumatics    $1.2M YTD spend
   #5  Emerson Automation    Control valves, sensors   $1.0M YTD spend

   Single-source exposure represents 38% of total Q1 spend — board
   directive is to reduce this to <25% by year-end.

4. WORKING CAPITAL & INVENTORY HEALTH

   Inventory turns ................. 6.8     (target 7.5)
   Days inventory outstanding ...... 53      (target 49)
   Slow-moving stock provision ..... $0.7M   (released $0.2M from Q4)

   Spare-parts inventory grew 4.1% sequentially driven by safety-stock
   build for the planned Line 3 PM in May. Action: review the $0.4M
   excess on legacy DC servo motors after the Line 2 cutover completes.

5. FORECAST & RISKS

   FY26 EBITDA guidance unchanged at $32-34M. Key downside risks:
     - Bearing tariff increase (10-15% additional duty) under review by USTR.
       Estimated annualised exposure: $1.1M.
     - Servo drive single-source dependency on Siemens — qualification of
       Yaskawa second-source is 60% complete, target Q3 closeout.
     - Labour contract renegotiation, July 2026. Provision of $0.8M
       per percentage point of wage increase above the 3.5% plan.
