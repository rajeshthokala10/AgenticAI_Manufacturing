Work Order: wo:asrs:416667
Subsystem: fuel_metering
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
a dc10 on dscnt at fl180 experienced a loss of pwr on #2 engine caused by a failed main fuel ctl unit.

## Resolution
AT APPROX FL180, 58 DME FROM LAX ON THE CIVET 4 PROFILE INTO LAX (ILS FREQ 109.9, 249 DEG RADIAL FOR A RWY 25L RWY LNDG) DSNDING, #2 ENG WOULD NOT DEVELOP NORMAL THRUST. #2 ENG FUEL FLOW WOULD NOT GO ABOVE 400 LBS PER HR WITH ANY THROTTLE MOVEMENT. CONTINUOUS IGNITION SELECTED. FLT ATTENDANT 'PREPARE FOR LNDG' WAS GIVEN. GIVEN THAT WE WERE IN DSCNT, WEIGHING 315000 LBS WITH BOTH #1 AND #3 ENGS DEVELOPING NORMAL THRUST, OUR CONDITION PRESENTED NO UNUSUAL PROBS. AT APPROX 28 DME AT 14000 FT MSL, ALL TANK FUEL BOOST PUMPS OPERATING, WE NOTED A ZERO FUEL FLOW ON #2 ENG. NO RESPONSE TO THROTTLE MOVEMENT. #2 GENERATOR LIGHT APPEARED. PRECAUTIONARY ENG SHUTDOWN CHKLIST ACCOMPLISHED, 2 ENG APCH AND LNDG, GAR NOTES REVIEWED. #2 ENG SECURED. ACFT ELECTRICAL SYS INTEGRITY NORMAL AND MONITORED. NORMAL LNDG TO RWY 25L LAX SUBSEQUENTLY ASSURED AND ACCOMPLISHED. CALLBACK CONVERSATION WITH RPTR REVEALED THE FOLLOWING INFO: THE RPTR STATED THE LOSS OF PWR ON #2 ENG WAS CAUSED BY A MAIN FUEL CTL FAILURE.
