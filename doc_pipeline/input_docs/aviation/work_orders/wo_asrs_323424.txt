Work Order: wo:asrs:323424
Subsystem: fuel_metering
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
rptr ran out of fuel to r engine and feathered the engine when it first started to sputter. the rptr made a safe single engine landing

## Resolution
I WAS FLYING A SAFETY PATROL FOR THE UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT. WE DEPARTED LNA AT XA40. I SWITCHED TO AUX TANKS AT XA50. AT THE TIME, THE 15 GAL AUX TANKS WERE FULL. MY EXPERIENCE IN THE ACFT INDICATED THAT FUEL BURN FOR BOTH ENGS AT PATROL PWR SETTINGS (18 INCHES MANIFOLD PRESSURE, 2300 RPM, LEANED 100 DEGS F RICH OF PEAK) WAS LESS THAN 15 GPH AND THE PERFORMANCE TABLES CERTAINLY CONFIRM THIS. WHAT I DID NOT KNOW WAS THAT THE 2 LYCOMING ID-320B ENGS DO NOT BURN FUEL AT THE SAME RATE. THE R ENG, AS IT TURNED OUT, BURNED 2 GPH MORE THAN THE L ENG. BECAUSE OF OUR LOW ALT, 1000 FT ABOVE THE OCEAN, I FEATHERED AND SECURED THE R ENG WHEN IT STARTED TO COUGH AND SPUTTER. WE MADE AN UNEVENTFUL SINGLE ENG LNDG AT KEY WEST WITH 45 GALS ON BOARD. BUT, THE R AUX TANK WAS COMPLETELY EMPTY.
