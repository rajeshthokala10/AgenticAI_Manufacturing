Work Order: wo:asrs:863182
Subsystem: fuel_metering
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
a line mechanic reports he responded to a call about an a320 on a gate, with a fuel pump fault light for the center pump switches 1 and 2, close to departure time. after reseting the fuel quantity indication computer (fqic) circuit breakers 1 and 2, he was distracted by a conversation with flight crew and failed to reset three other center right pump c/b's.

## Resolution
December 2009, got a call from Maintenance Control about an A320 aircraft fuel pump fault. I went on the call to see what was going on, it was close to departure time. There was a fault light on center (ctr) pump switches 1 and 2. 

I pulled the Fuel Quantity Indication Computer (FQIC) 1 and 2 Circuit Breaker (C/B) and Center pump (Right) R-25, R-26, R-27. I reset FQIC-1 and -2 C/B, and the Fault went away. I got distracted talking to the flight crew and left the flight deck. Later on I realized I left R-25, R-26, R-27 Circuit Breakers open.
