Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
be35 pilot reports flying a new engine break in flight at 5,500 feet and returning. the next flight is planned at 500 agl to increase manifold pressure. when cht begins to rise few options remain.

## Resolution
The average terrain elevation in this location may make this break-in procedure inadvisable. A rebuilt engine offers many opportunities for mistakes that can cause the engine to fail. Staying within gliding range of an airport is usually desirable.
