Subsystem: both
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
aeronca 7ac, champ, student plt experience rough running engine and pwr loss for short time resulting in the plt circling a golf course in case a forced landing was necessary. in addition, his handheld radio batteries were low preventing him from declaring an emer. he stayed over land and was able to return to his home base.

## Resolution
DEPARTED NORTH PERRY ARPT (HWO) ON NOV/XA/01. TRAVELING SE DOWN MIAMI BEACH, APPROX 15 MI INTO FLT, MY ENG STARTED RUNNING ROUGH AND BACKFIRED SEVERAL TIMES. LOSS OF RPM -- I LOST APPROX 300 FT ALT. AT THIS TIME I TRIED TO CONTACT MIA APCH, BUT BATTERY IN HANDHELD RADIO FAILED. AFTER CIRCLING MIAMI BEACH GOLF COURSE FOR POSSIBLE LNDG, MY ENG REGAINED RPM. FOR SAFETY MEASURES, I REMAINED OVER LAND, PROCEEDED S FOR SEVERAL MI UNTIL I WAS CERTAIN ENG WOULD CONTINUE OPERATING TO NORTH PERRY ARPT. AT THIS TIME I CHANGED BATTERIES IN RADIO AND PROCEEDED BACK TO NORTH PERRY ARPT. CONTRIBUTING FACTORS: LOSS OF RADIO DUE TO BATTERY FAILURE. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS: CHANGED BATTERIES -- SPARE. PERCEPTIONS, JUDGEMENTS, DECISIONS: CIRCLED POSSIBLE LNDG ZONE. ACTIONS INCLUDED CHKING MAGNETO SET TO 'BOTH,' CHKED FUEL SELECT SWITCH -- ALL GAUGES. FACTORS AFFECTING HUMAN PERFORMANCE: LOW TIME STUDENT PLT.
