Work Order: wo:asrs:427008
Subsystem: fuel_metering
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
plt of c150 experiences engine failure near austin.

## Resolution
THIS FLT WAS A DIRECT RTE FROM LAKEWAY AIRPARK NEAR AUSTIN, TX, TO HONDO MUNICIPAL ARPT, A DISTANCE OF ABOUT 90 MI. AFTER TOPPING OFF FUEL AND OTHER USUAL PREPARATIONS, WE TOOK OFF AT XA15 AND CLBED TO 4500 FT ON COURSE. ABOUT 35 MINS LATER, THE ENG SUDDENLY SPUTTERED. I APPLIED CARB HEAT, BUT RPM KEPT DETERIORATING UNTIL ALL PWR WAS LOST. THE ENG WINDMILLED BUT WOULD NOT RESTART. I CALLED BULVERDE ARPT ON UNICOM -- NO ANSWER. I BROADCAST ON 121.5 -- NO RESPONSE. APPROX 5 MI TO MY R WAS THE MIL AUX FIELD AT CAMP BULLIS, TX. WE GLIDED TO THIS MIL FIELD AND LANDED ON THE RWY THERE. THE ACFT WAS NOT DAMAGED AND NEITHER ME NOR MY WIFE (PAX) WERE INJURED. THIS WAS A RENTED ACFT. WE LEFT OUR POINT OF DEP WITH FULL FUEL TANKS (GAS SLIP AVAILABLE). ENG INSTS WERE NORMAL (GOOD OIL PRESSURE) WHEN THE ENG QUIT. I DID NOT HEAR ANY CLANKING OR ROUGHNESS WHEN THE ENG QUIT AS IS SOMETIMES ASSOCIATED WITH AN INTERNAL FAILURE. AT THIS TIME I DO NOT KNOW THE CAUSE OF THE ENG FAILURE. I DID NOT LAND AT THE BULVERDE ARPT BECAUSE IT WAS 8-10 MI TO MY L. CAMP BULLIS WAS MUCH CLOSER AND OBVIOUSLY THE CORRECT CHOICE.
