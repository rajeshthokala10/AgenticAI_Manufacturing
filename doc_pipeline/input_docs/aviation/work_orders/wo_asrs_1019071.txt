Work Order: wo:asrs:1019071
Subsystem: fuel_metering
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
a line mechanic reports the left engine on a company b767-300 aircraft had shutdown on landing when the thrust reversers were deployed. a metal gasket he had installed between the fuel/oil cooler housing and the fuel "in" tube connection to the fuel/oil cooler had failed.

## Resolution
Reporter's event once again demonstrates the limited procedures or resources Mechanics have available to verify that fuel, oil or hydraulic seal replacements have been properly installed. Engine Operational Checks are generally limited to Ground Idle Engine Runs. Fuel/Oil Coolers are generally mounted on the Fan Case section of PW 2000, PW 4000 engines, CFM-56 and Airbus V-2500 engines, compared to the Fuel/Oil Cooler location on the Combustor section on GE CF6 engines.
