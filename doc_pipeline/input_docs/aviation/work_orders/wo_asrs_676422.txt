Work Order: wo:asrs:676422
Subsystem: fuel_metering
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
a kitfox iv incurred damage when on approach to a short field, plt lost throttle ctl and made a hard landing with a gnd loop.

## Resolution
THROTTLE CONNECTION ON HOME BUILT WAS NOT SATISFACTORY TO ENSURE SAFETY AND ACFT RECEIVED DAMAGE, BUT NO INJURIES OCCURRED. IT APPEARS THAT THE LOCK NUT ON THE TURNBUCKLE WAS NOT SATISFACTORY AND ADDITIONAL SAFETY WIRE SHOULD HAVE BEEN USED. THE THROTTLE BECAME DISCONNECTED ON FINAL APCH TO LNDG, RESULTING IN A HARD LNDG AND SOME DAMAGE TO THE ACFT. CALLBACK CONVERSATION WITH RPTR REVEALED THE FOLLOWING INFO: THE RPTR STATED WITH THE LOSS OF THE THROTTLE CONNECTION, THE AIRPLANE HAD TOO MUCH PWR FOR LNDG BUT NOT ENOUGH PWR TO GO AROUND. THE CAUSE WAS THE LOCK NUT ON THE THROTTLE LINKAGE BACKED OFF AND THROTTLE CTL WAS LOST. THE AIRPLANE WAS HARD LANDED AND THEN GND LOOPED, INCURRING DAMAGE TO THE WING SPARS. THE INCIDENT OCCURRED AT A PVT FIELD. THE AIRPLANE IS A HOME BUILT KIT AIRPLANE BUT WAS NOT BUILT BY THE RPTR.
