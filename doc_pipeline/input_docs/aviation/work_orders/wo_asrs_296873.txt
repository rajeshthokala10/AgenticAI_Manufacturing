Work Order: wo:asrs:296873
Subsystem: ignition
Source: ASRS (Aviation Safety Reporting System)

## Complaint
off arpt landing due to reciprocating engine failure.

## Resolution
IN CRUISE FROM TKI TO ADS, ENG FAILED. PERFORMED EMER RESTART PROCS (NO RESTART). CALLED 'MAYDAY, MAYDAY, NXXXX ENG FAILURE, GOING DOWN' TO ADDISON TWR. PICKED BEST SUITABLE FIELD FOR EMER LNDG. ATTEMPTED RESTART AGAIN AND DETERMINED ENG WOULD NOT START. SECURED AIRPLANE FOR EMER LNDG. TOUCHED DOWN IN FIELD, HELD NOSE OFF AS LONG AS POSSIBLE. WHEN NOSEWHEEL DUG INTO THE RAIN-SOFTENED FIELD, IT BROKE OFF AND ACFT FLIPPED OVER. IMMEDIATELY EVACED ACFT. NO INJURIES. CALLBACK CONVERSATION WITH RPTR REVEALED THE FOLLOWING INFO: THE RPTR WAS FLYING A CESSNA 172RG WITH AN O-320 ENG. A PIN IN THE ACCESSORY GEAR DRIVE SHEARED DISABLING BOTH MAGNETOS AND THE VALVE TRAIN. THE ENG WINDMILLED THROUGHOUT THE EMER. THERE WAS MINIMAL DAMAGE TO THE ACFT IN ADDITION TO THE NOSE GEAR. THE RPTR IS CONCERNED ABOUT A COUNTERFEIT PART HAVING BEEN PUT INTO THIS ENG. IT HAS NOT BEEN DETERMINED IF THIS WAS THE CASE HERE.
