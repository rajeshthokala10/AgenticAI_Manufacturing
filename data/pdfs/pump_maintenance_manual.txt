1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This manual provides maintenance procedures, troubleshooting guidelines, and
spare parts information for the P-200 series centrifugal pumps installed at the
Greenfield Manufacturing Facility. All maintenance personnel must review this
document before performing any service on P-200 series equipment.

Related documents:
  - PLC Controls Reference (DOC-PLC-500-REV03)
  - Conveyor System Guide (MAN-CV300-REV02) — see Section 7 for P-203 cooling
  - Hydraulic Press Manual (MAN-HP400-REV03)

  - Wear rings: FT-WR-200-F (front) / FT-WR-200-R (rear)
  - Impeller nut: FT-NUT-200, torque to 85 ft-lbs

3.2 Mechanical Seal
  - Type: John Crane 2100 Single Cartridge
  - Part No: JC-2100-1.750 (1.750" shaft)
  - Seal face: Silicon Carbide vs. Carbon
  - Elastomers: Viton (standard) / EPDM (P-201 water service)
  - Flush Plan: API Plan 11 (P-201, P-203) / API Plan 32 (P-202)
  - Flush pressure: 15-25 psi above suction pressure

3.3 Bearing Housing Assembly
  - Housing: FT-BH-200 cast iron housing
  - Inboard bearing: SKF 6310-2RS (Part: SKF-6310-2RS)
  - Outboard bearing: SKF 6311-2RS (Part: SKF-6311-2RS)
  - Lubricant: Mobil Polyrex EM grease
  - Oil capacity (if oil-lubricated variant): 1.2 liters
  - Bearing temperature limit: 180°F (82°C) continuous

3.4 Coupling
  - Type: Rexnord Omega E30 elastomeric
  - Part No: REX-E30-28 (28mm bore)
  - Element: REX-E30-ELEM (polyurethane, orange — Shore 80A)
  - Alignment tolerance: 0.005" parallel, 0.5° angular
  - Torque rating: 450 ft-lbs maximum

3.5 Motor
  - See Section 2 for motor specifications
  - Motor-to-pump alignment is critical; see Section 6.3
  - VFD controlled: P-201 (Siemens G120C, 25HP)
  - Across-the-line start: P-202, P-203
  - Motor PLC I/O mapped in PLC Controls Reference, Section 4

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Code      | Description                          | Severity | Action
----------|--------------------------------------|----------|------------------
ALM-P001  | High vibration — inboard bearing      | Warning  | Check alignment
ALM-P002  | High vibration — outboard bearing      | Warning  | Check bearing
ALM-P003  | Seal leak detected (flush flow low)    | Critical | Stop pump
ALM-P004  | Bearing temperature high (>170°F)      | Warning  | Reduce load
ALM-P005  | Bearing temperature critical (>190°F)  | Critical | Stop pump
ALM-P006  | Low discharge pressure                 | Warning  | Check suction

5. TROUBLESHOOTING PROCEDURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 HIGH VIBRATION (ALM-P001, ALM-P002)

Probable Causes:
  a) Misalignment between pump and motor
  b) Worn or damaged bearings
  c) Impeller imbalance (erosion, debris)
  d) Cavitation
  e) Loose foundation bolts

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Record vibration levels using portable analyzer (CSI 2140 or equivalent).
     - Acceptable: < 0.15 in/s velocity (overall)
     - Warning: 0.15 — 0.30 in/s
     - Critical: > 0.30 in/s
  2. Check foundation bolts for proper torque (3/4" bolts: 150 ft-lbs).
  3. Perform laser alignment check using Fixturlaser NXA Pro.
     - Parallel tolerance: 0.005"
     - Angular tolerance: 0.5°
  4. If alignment is within tolerance, check bearing condition:
     - Measure bearing temperature with IR thermometer.
     - Listen for bearing noise using stethoscope.
     - If defective, replace per Section 6.2.
  5. If bearings are acceptable, inspect impeller:
     - Remove casing cover per Section 6.1.
     - Check impeller for erosion, pitting, or debris.
     - Replace impeller if wear exceeds 1/16" on vane edges.

5.2 SEAL LEAKAGE (ALM-P003)

Probable Causes:
  a) Seal face wear (normal end-of-life)
  b) Thermal shock from dry running
  c) Flush system malfunction
  d) Shaft runout exceeding 0.002" TIR

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Isolate pump: close suction and discharge valves.
  2. Verify LOTO per facility procedure LP-007.
  3. Inspect seal flush system:
     - Verify flush flow: 1.5-2.0 GPM at gauge FG-P2xx-01.
     - Verify flush pressure: 15-25 psi above suction.
     - Clean strainer (Part: FT-STR-200) if clogged.
  4. If flush system is functional, replace seal cartridge:
     - Remove coupling guard and coupling half.
     - Slide back gland plate (4x M12 bolts).
     - Remove old cartridge and inspect shaft sleeve.
     - Measure shaft runout with dial indicator (max 0.002" TIR).
     - Install new JC-2100 cartridge per manufacturer instructions.
     - Set seal compression: 3/16" gap on setting clips.
     - Torque gland bolts to 25 ft-lbs in star pattern.

5.3 LOW FLOW / LOW DISCHARGE PRESSURE (ALM-P006)

Probable Causes:
  a) Suction strainer clogged
  b) Partially closed suction valve
  c) Air entrainment in suction line
  d) Worn impeller or wear rings
  e) Reverse rotation (after motor work)

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Verify suction valve is fully open.
  2. Check suction strainer differential pressure (DP > 5 psi = clogged).
  3. Check for air leaks at suction piping flanges and gauge connections.
  4. Verify rotation direction: viewed from motor end, CCW for all P-200 pumps.
  5. Measure wear ring clearance:
     - Acceptable: 0.010 — 0.015" diametral
     - Replace at: > 0.025" diametral
  6. If impeller is worn beyond tolerance, replace per Section 6.1.

5.4 OVERHEATING (ALM-P004, ALM-P005)

Probable Causes:
  a) Insufficient lubrication
  b) Over-greasing (grease churning)
  c) Misalignment causing excessive bearing load
  d) Ambient temperature exceeding design
  e) Cooling water flow loss (P-203: check valve CV-P203-CW)

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Immediately reduce pump load or stop pump if temperature exceeds 190°F.
  2. Check grease fill level: bearings should be 1/3 full.
  3. If over-greased, remove drain plug and run pump briefly to purge excess.
  4. Verify bearing housing is not insulated by debris or dirt buildup.
  5. Check alignment per Section 5.1 step 3.
  6. For P-203: verify cooling water supply from plant header is flowing.

5.5 CAVITATION (ALM-P008)

Probable Causes:
  a) Insufficient NPSH available
  b) Suction lift too high
  c) Fluid temperature above design (reduced NPSH margin)
  d) Suction line restrictions

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Measure suction pressure at gauge PG-P2xx-S1.
  2. Calculate NPSHa and compare to NPSHr:
     - P-201 NPSHr: 8 ft at BEP
     - P-202 NPSHr: 12 ft at BEP
     - P-203 NPSHr: 9 ft at BEP
  3. Ensure NPSHa exceeds NPSHr by minimum 3 ft margin.
  4. Check suction strainer for blockage.
  5. Verify fluid temperature is within design range.
  6. If cavitation persists, consider throttling discharge to reduce flow.


6. MAINTENANCE PROCEDURES AND INTERVALS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE

Task                                  | Interval   | Procedure
--------------------------------------|------------|---------------------------
Visual inspection and leak check      | Daily      | Walk-down checklist WD-P200
Vibration reading (portable)          | Weekly     | Record in CMMS
Bearing temperature check             | Weekly     | IR thermometer
Seal flush flow verification          | Weekly     | Check gauge FG-P2xx-01
Coupling element inspection           | Monthly    | Visual, check for cracking
Suction strainer cleaning             | Monthly    | Isolate, remove, clean
Lubrication (grease bearings)         | Quarterly  | 2 pumps Mobil Polyrex EM
Alignment check                       | Semi-annual| Laser alignment
Seal replacement (planned)            | 18 months  | Full cartridge swap
Bearing replacement (planned)         | 24 months  | Both inboard & outboard
Full overhaul (casing, impeller)      | 48 months  | Complete disassembly

6.2 BEARING REPLACEMENT PROCEDURE

  1. Perform LOTO per facility procedure LP-007.
  2. Disconnect motor leads and coupling.
  3. Remove bearing housing end caps (4x M10 bolts each).
  4. Extract bearings using SKF TMMA 100 puller kit.
  5. Inspect shaft for scoring or wear.
  6. Clean housing bore with lint-free cloth and solvent.
  7. Heat new bearings to 110°C using SKF TIH 030m induction heater.
  8. Install bearings with marked side facing outward.
  9. Pack with Mobil Polyrex EM grease (1/3 cavity fill).
  10. Reassemble and torque end cap bolts to 45 ft-lbs.
  11. Verify shaft rotates freely by hand.
  12. Reconnect coupling and perform laser alignment.

6.3 ALIGNMENT PROCEDURE

  1. Ensure pump and motor are at operating temperature (warm alignment).
  2. Mount Fixturlaser NXA Pro brackets on coupling hubs.
  3. Rotate shafts and record readings at 12, 3, 6, 9 o'clock.
  4. Calculate corrections for front and rear motor feet.
  5. Add or remove shims (stainless steel shim stock only).
  6. Re-measure and confirm within tolerance:
     - Parallel: 0.005" maximum
     - Angular: 0.5° maximum
  7. Torque motor hold-down bolts to 120 ft-lbs.
  8. Perform soft-foot check (max 0.002" lift per foot).

8. SAFETY INFORMATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Always perform Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) per procedure LP-007 before any
    maintenance activity.
  - P-202 handles chemical fluids — wear appropriate PPE including chemical-
    resistant gloves, face shield, and acid-resistant apron.
  - Verify zero energy state before opening any casing or piping.
  - Do not operate pumps with coupling guard removed.
  - Maximum casing temperature during operation: 120°C — use caution.
  - Report any seal leaks immediately to shift supervisor and environmental.
