1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This guide covers the installation, operation, and maintenance of the CV-300
series belt conveyor systems at the Greenfield Manufacturing Facility. The
CV-300 series comprises three conveyor lines integrated into the production
workflow and controlled by Siemens S7-1500 and Allen-Bradley CompactLogix PLCs.

Related documents:
  - PLC Controls Reference (DOC-PLC-500-REV03)
  - Pump Maintenance Manual (MAN-P200-REV04) — P-203 cooling for CV-303
  - Hydraulic Press Manual (MAN-HP400-REV03) — HP-401 feed integration

3. COMPONENT BREAKDOWN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Belt Assembly
  - Part: CPX-BELT-36 (36" width) / CPX-BELT-24 / CPX-BELT-30
  - Splice type: Vulcanized finger splice (factory) / mechanical Flexco SR
  - Minimum pulley diameter: 16" (head/tail) / 8" (snub)
  - Belt tracking adjustment: Crown on head pulley + adjustable snub rollers

3.2 Rollers and Idlers
  - Carrying idlers: CPX-IDL-C36 / CPX-IDL-C24 / CPX-IDL-C30
  - Return idlers: CPX-IDL-R36 / CPX-IDL-R24 / CPX-IDL-R30
  - Impact idlers (loading zone): CPX-IDL-IMP-36 (CV-301 only)
  - Bearing type: 6205-2RS sealed ball bearing
  - Idler shell: ERW steel tube, 5" diameter

3.3 Drive Motor and Gearbox
  - Motor: WEG W22 (see specs in Section 2)
  - Gearbox: SEW R87 DRE series with torque arm mount
  - Gearbox oil: SEW GearOil CLP 220, capacity 3.5 liters
  - Oil change interval: 12 months or 10,000 operating hours

3.4 Belt Tensioner
  - Type: Screw take-up (CV-302) / Gravity take-up (CV-301, CV-303)
  - Gravity counterweight: 350 lbs (CV-301) / 250 lbs (CV-303)
  - Take-up travel: 36" maximum
  - Part: CPX-TU-GRAV-300 (gravity) / CPX-TU-SCREW-300 (screw)

3.5 Sensors and Instrumentation
  - Belt speed sensor: Honeywell 942-A4M-2D (magnetic pickup)
    Part: HON-942-A4M | Mounted on tail pulley
  - Belt misalignment switch: 4B Touchswitch TS-300
    Part: 4B-TS300 | Mounted both sides at head and tail
  - Pull-cord E-stop: Allen-Bradley 440E Lifeline 4
    Part: AB-440E-LL4 | Every 50 ft along conveyor
  - Load cell (CV-301): HBM PW15AH, 500 kg capacity
    Part: HBM-PW15AH | Mounted at weigh frame station
  - Photoelectric sensor (product detect): Banner Q45 series
    Part: BAN-Q45-18 | At load zone and discharge
  - Proximity sensors (diverter gates, CV-303): IFM IF5343
    Part: IFM-IF5343 | 2 per gate (open/close confirmation)

3.6 Variable Frequency Drive (VFD)
  - Model: Siemens SINAMICS G120C, frame size FSC
  - Power ratings: 10HP (CV-301), 5HP (CV-302), 7.5HP (CV-303)
  - Control mode: Vector control with encoder feedback
  - Communication: PROFINET IO with 2-port switch
  - Parameters configured per PLC Controls Reference, Appendix B


4. ALARM CODES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Code      | Description                          | Severity | Action
----------|--------------------------------------|----------|------------------
ALM-C001  | Belt misalignment — head end          | Warning  | Adjust tracking
ALM-C002  | Belt misalignment — tail end          | Warning  | Adjust tracking
ALM-C003  | Drive motor overtemperature            | Critical | Stop conveyor
ALM-C004  | Belt speed deviation (>10% from SP)    | Warning  | Check belt slip
ALM-C005  | E-stop activated                      | Critical | Investigate
ALM-C006  | VFD fault (general)                   | Critical | Check VFD code

VFD Sub-Fault Codes (displayed on SINAMICS G120C):
  - F07801: Motor overcurrent
  - F07802: Motor stall detected
  - F07900: Drive overtemperature
  - F30001: PROFINET communication loss (see PLC Reference, Section 6)
  - F30002: Encoder signal lost

Alarm routing: Conveyor alarms are mapped to the Siemens S7-1500 PLC via
PROFINET. HMI display on Siemens TP1500 Comfort Panel at operator station
OS-CV300. Critical alarms trigger the area beacon (red strobe + horn).

For HMI alarm display configuration, see PLC Controls Reference, Section 5.


5. TROUBLESHOOTING PROCEDURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 BELT TRACKING / MISALIGNMENT (ALM-C001, ALM-C002)

Probable Causes:
  a) Uneven loading on belt
  b) Material buildup on rollers or pulleys
  c) Belt splice not square
  d) Idler misalignment
  e) Take-up not centered

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Observe belt tracking while running at low speed (30 ft/min).
  2. Inspect for material buildup on head pulley, snub rollers, and return
     idlers. Clean with scraper if buildup found.
  3. Check that all carrying idlers are level and square to belt centerline.
     Use a string line along the conveyor frame as reference.
  4. Adjust snub rollers at head or tail:
     - Belt drifting right: move roller right end forward (in direction of travel).
     - Belt drifting left: move roller left end forward.
     - Adjust in 1/8" increments, wait 3 full belt revolutions between changes.
  5. Verify belt splice is square: measure diagonals across splice — should
     be equal within 1/4".
  6. Confirm take-up (gravity or screw) is centered and free to move.
  7. If belt continues to mistrack after all adjustments, belt may be
     damaged or stretched unevenly — schedule belt replacement.

5.2 MOTOR FAULTS (ALM-C003, VFD sub-faults)

Probable Causes:
  a) Motor overload due to belt jam or material buildup
  b) VFD parameter drift or corruption
  c) Motor winding degradation
  d) Insufficient cooling airflow

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Check VFD display panel for specific fault code (F-codes listed above).
  2. For F07801 (overcurrent):
     - Inspect belt for jams or excessive material accumulation.
     - Check that discharge chute is not blocked.
     - Verify VFD current limit parameter (P0640) is set to 150% FLA.
  3. For F07900 (drive overtemperature):
     - Check VFD cooling fan operation.
     - Verify ambient temperature in drive cabinet < 40°C.
     - Clean VFD heatsink fins with compressed air.
  4. For F30001 (PROFINET loss):
     - Check PROFINET cable connections at VFD and PLC.
     - Verify network switch status (see PLC Reference, Section 6).
     - Ping VFD IP address from engineering workstation.
     - VFD IP addresses: CV-301: 192.168.1.31, CV-302: 192.168.1.32,
       CV-303: 192.168.1.33
  5. If motor winding fault suspected, perform insulation resistance test
     (megger) — minimum 50 Megohms at 500V DC.
  6. Reset VFD fault by cycling control power or pressing RESET on keypad.
     If fault recurs within 3 attempts, escalate to electrical maintenance.

5.3 SENSOR FAILURES

5.3.1 Belt Speed Sensor Failure
  - Symptom: Speed reading = 0 while belt is running, or erratic readings.
  - Check: Gap between sensor and target wheel (0.5-1.5mm).
  - Check: Sensor cable for damage or disconnection at terminal block TB-CV3xx.
  - Verify: 24VDC supply at sensor (measure at TB-CV3xx terminals 1 & 2).
  - Replace: Part HON-942-A4M, recalibrate PPR setting in PLC.

5.3.2 Belt Misalignment Switch
  - Symptom: False ALM-C001/C002 while belt is tracking correctly.
  - Check: Switch lever arm free movement, not stuck by debris.
  - Check: Switch contacts with multimeter (NC contact should read closed).
  - Adjust: Switch mounting position — lever should contact belt edge at
    1" deviation from centerline.
  - Replace: Part 4B-TS300 if contacts are worn.

5.3.3 Photoelectric Sensor (Product Detect)
  - Symptom: Missed product detection or false triggers.
  - Check: Sensor lens for dust or debris — clean with soft cloth.
  - Check: Reflector alignment (retroreflective type).
  - Adjust: Sensitivity potentiometer on sensor body.
  - Verify: 24VDC supply and output signal at PLC input module.

5.4 SPEED DEVIATIONS (ALM-C004)

Probable Causes:
  a) Belt slippage on drive pulley
  b) VFD speed reference signal error
  c) Gearbox failure (internal gear wear)
  d) Motor slip exceeding nameplate

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Compare VFD output frequency to speed sensor feedback.
  2. If VFD output matches setpoint but belt speed is low:
     - Inspect drive pulley rubber lagging for wear (replace if <1/8" thick).
     - Check belt tension: gravity take-up should move freely; screw take-up
       should be adjusted to eliminate slip (belt sag between idlers: 2-3%).
  3. If VFD output does not match speed reference:
     - Check analog speed reference signal from PLC (4-20mA at AO-CV3xx-01).
     - Verify VFD analog input scaling (P2000-P2003).
  4. Check gearbox oil level and condition:
     - Oil should be clear amber; milky = water contamination.
     - Dark/burnt = overheating; metallic particles = gear wear.
     - If gear wear suspected, schedule gearbox replacement.

8. SAFETY INFORMATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Never reach under a running conveyor belt. All work on belt, rollers,
    or drive requires LOTO per facility procedure LP-007.
  - Pull-cord E-stops are located every 50 ft. Know the nearest E-stop
    location before working near any conveyor.
  - Conveyor guards must be in place during operation. Do not bypass guard
    interlocks (Allen-Bradley 440G-MT Guard Locking Switch).
  - Pinch points exist at head pulley, tail pulley, snub rollers, and
    take-up. Maintain 24" clearance zone.
  - CV-301 is interlocked with hydraulic press HP-401. Verify both systems
    are in safe state before performing maintenance on either.
  - Maximum walking speed near conveyors: normal pace. No running.
